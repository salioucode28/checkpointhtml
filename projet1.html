I. ABOUT

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. Born in Ulm, Germany in 1879, he developed the theory of relativity — a groundbreaking framework that transformed our understanding of space, time, and gravity. His famous equation, E = mc², became a symbol of scientific genius and revolutionized modern physics. Driven by curiosity and a deep sense of wonder, Einstein combined creativity with scientific rigor. In 1921, he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which paved the way for quantum theory. Beyond science, Einstein was a passionate advocate for peace, civil rights, and humanitarian values. His legacy continues to inspire generations of thinkers, dreamers, and innovators across the globe.

II. WORK

  • Theory of relativity (1905-1915)

    A revolutionary framework that reshaped modern physics. Includes the Special Theory of Relativity, introducing the famous equation E = mc², and the General Theory of Relativity, which redefined our understanding of gravity. Technologies: Thought Experiments, Mathematics, Theoretical Physics

  • Photoelectric Effect (1905)

    Demonstrated how light can be understood as discrete packets of energy (photons), laying the foundation for quantum theory. This breakthrough earned him the Nobel Prize in Physics (1921).
    Technologies:     Quantum Theory, Light Physics, Electromagnetic Radiation

  • Einstein's Papers of 1905 - Annus Mirabilis

    Published four groundbreaking papers in one year on:

    • Brownian motion
    • photoelectric Effect
    • Special Relativity
    • Mass-Energy Equivalence
    These works collectively changed the course of modern science.
    Technologies: Scientific research, Analitical Reasoning, Problem Solving

  • Advocacy & Public Engagement

    • Einstein wasn’t just a scientist — he was a global citizen. He used his voice to promote peace, civil rights, and freedom of thought. He opposed war and racial injustice and supported the creation of institutions for global cooperation. Focus Areas: Ethics, Human Rights, Diplomacy
